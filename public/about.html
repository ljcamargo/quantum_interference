About Quantum Interference


Quantum Interference is an artistic exploration of quantum probability amplitude fields. This simulator maps the complex statevector of a multi-qubit system into a physical interference pattern, allowing users to visualize the mathematical heart of quantum computing.


How it Works


Each computational basis state is mapped to a spatial coordinate. The probability amplitude (a complex number) of each state governs the magnitude and phase of a wave source at that coordinate. The final image is the result of thousands of waves interfering constructively and destructively across the space.


The Gates


  • H (Hadamard): Creates superposition, the key to interference patterns.
  • X (Pauli-X): Flips states, shifting the "energy" or focus of the field.
  • S & T (Phase): Rotates the phase of a source, changing the color and drift of the waves.
  • CNOT & CCNOT: Creates entanglement, coupling the sources in complex, correlated ways.
